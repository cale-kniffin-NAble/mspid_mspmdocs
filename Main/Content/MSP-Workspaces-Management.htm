Create and manage Workspaces

Workspaces are collections of workspace pages. Propose built workspaces act as templates and can be active, in preview or inactive.

https://projects.invisionapp.com/share/E3DBO00HX#/screens/261082817

Workspaces are collections of WorkspacePages that host "WorkspaceWidgets"

1. A system workspace is a "purpose-built" workspace.

2. It acts as a "template"

3. It may only be edited by a SuperUser, with permission to Manage System Workspaces

4. A System Workspace is [InActive, InPreview, Active]

5. A Inactive System Workspace may only be Edited by the SuperUser. It may be SEEN by any SuperUser.

6. A InPreview System Workspace may be seen by any MSP, but may not be copied (it is essentially beta)

7. A Active System Workspace may be SEEN and COPIED by any MSP.

9. When a SystemWorkspace is Edited, the CRUD must operate on ALL shards.

10. When the NAV-BAR opens to Workspaces, there are 3 sections: [User (the individual user - ie mine), MSP (the MSPs), System (the SystemWorkspaces).

(note: At this point, I would like to defer WorkspacePortal)

11. All MSPs see the SAME WorkspaceSystem "template" workspaces.

12. Separate schema is required for SystemWorkspaces

Workspace_MSP

1. A MSP may design a workspace specifically for his MSP.

2. He may do this 2 ways: COPY from a SYSTEM workspace, or CREATE a new Workspace from scratch, or COPY from a Workspace_User to a new Workspace.

3. When he SAAVES the Workspace, he designates this as a "MSP" workspace.

4. Typically, a Workspace_MSP is a "view-only" workspace, and MAY NOT be edited.

(note: This MAY cause issues related to changing filters etc)

5. In NAVBAR, WorkspaceMSP appear AFTER the Workspace_User in the menu selector.

6. WorkspaceMSP are stored in the main Workspace schema.

7. There are permissions to Manage MSP level workspaces.
