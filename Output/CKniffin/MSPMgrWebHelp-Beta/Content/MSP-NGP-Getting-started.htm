MSP Manager

Get started with MSP Manager

MSP Manager is cloud-based service management software that helps you support customers and run an effective IT service business.

Organize and manage all of your customers' information in one place, including locations, contacts, program levels, service items, knowledge, and billing information.

Get started by adding new users

Navigate to Settings > Users & Permissions > Users > Add User, and enter the new user's information.

Select Enable Two Factor Authentication to activate Multifactor Authentication for the user.

To remove users from your account, make sure they do not have any tickets or appointments assigned to them. As an alternative to removing or deleting a user, you can also mark them Inactive. Neither Inactive or Deleted users are billed for. Navigate to the Users section, select a user to edit, clear the checkbox in the Status section of the User Editor and save the User to deactivate their account.

You can also create Roles for MSP Manager users, and assign them to Access Groups to limit the user's access to a specific group of customers.

Add new customers to your account

Use the New Customer Setup wizard to add customers to your account, create program levels and service item contracts, and assign locations and contacts to the new customers.

  1. Navigate to > Add Customer.
  2. Enter the customer's General information, including one or more email domains for the customer and any Customer Access Groups you would like the new customer to be part of.
  3. In the Notification section, select the Language and Email Format for the customer's notifications, and choose a mailbox from the Send email from field. This is the monitored mailbox that sends notifications to that specific customer.
  4. Enter their Advanced Settings(optional), and click Next.

    5. Add assigned users to customers and configure your Ticket Routing rules to automatically assign inbound tickets to those specific users.

  5. Select a Program Level and click Next.
  6. View the Service Items generated by your Program Level, and Add customer specific Service Items to the Program Level if needed. Click Next.
  7. Select Add Locations and enter the customer's location name and information. Select to save the location and click Next.
  8. Select Add Contact to create a contact for the new customer. Select Portal User or Portal Admin to grant the contact access to your Customer Portal. Select to save the contact and click Next.

    9. Creating a Portal User will send an activation email to the contact with information on how to access and log in to the portal.

    When adding a Contact to a customer, leave the Location field blank to create a Customer Level Contact, which assigns the contact to the customer and not to a specific location.

  9. Click Finish to view the new customer's information from the Customer Dashboard.

The new customer information, Program Level, Service Items, Locations, and Contacts are saved to your account.

To edit existing customers, select the customer and click Actions > Edit Customer.

Configure your Company Settings

Make sure to configure your company settings, such as time zone, culture and company business hours before getting started with MSP Manager.

  1. Navigate to Settings > Company Settings > General.
  2. Select the Timezone in which your company operates and choose a Culture option to adjust the language and currency of your MSP Manager account.
  3. Enter the Tax Rate for the country, state, city or territory where your business is located.
  4. Enter a Time Entry Rounding option to round up time entries to the nearest specified increment.
  5. Specify a value for Grace Period Rounding. This is used to round small amounts of time down to the nearest selected time segment if the total time exceeds the rounded time value by less than the Grace Period value.

    6. Grace Period Rounding will only be applied after the first full time segment has been reached. For instance, if Time Entry Rounding is set to a quarter hour and Grace Period Rounding is set to 5 minutes, and you work on a ticket for 17 minutes, that time entry will round down to a quarter hour since it did not exceed the Time Entry Rounding value by more than 5 minutes (the Grace Period Rounding value).

  6. Enter your Next Ticket Number to change your ticket numbering sequence. This number can not be smaller than your current and latest ticket number.
  7. Select your business days and specify your business hours. This affects your SLA policies, as SLAs are put on hold during off-hours.
  8. Click Save Settings when finished.
