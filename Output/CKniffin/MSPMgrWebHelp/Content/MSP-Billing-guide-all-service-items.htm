MSP Manager

Service items and billing

Track your time by adding time entries to a ticket. Complete the ticket and create a new billing batch when you are ready to bill your customer. Tickets are grouped by customer and service item when creating a billing batch. Select one or more service items to add it to the batch to bill for all completed tickets under those service item. Create multiple tickets for any service item and bill them all at once in a single billing batch.

Hourly retainer

Hourly retainer service items generate a block of hours for your customer to use over the course of a month, quarter, or an entire year. Customers pay for the entire block of hours at the start of the billing period, and hours are deducted from the retainer as you open tickets and track time. Send an invoice each month for a new block of hours, and charge an separate rate for overage if your customer exceeds their allotted hours.

  1. Click Customers > Select a customer in the list.
  2. Select Service items > Add a New Service Item > Hour Retainer.



  3. Select a Retainer Period - this determines how often hours will accrue for billing and reporting purposes, and also when a new block of hours gets created.
  4. Select a Period Grouping option - this allows you to group together billing periods to share hours.
    1. For example, a 10 hour monthly retainer creates a block of 10 hours for your customer every month.
    2. Select Quarterly as the period grouping option to share the hours over the entire quarter (30 hours over 3 months). The customer can use up to 30 hours anytime during the quarter without being charged an overage rate.
    3. Your customer still receives an invoice each month for 10 hours, and overage is incurred if they exceed 30 hours before the end of the quarter.
  5. Specify a Retainer Amount, and Retainer Hours.

    6. This automatically calculates the Base Rate of the retainer. For example: $1000 Retainer Amount and 10 Retainer Hours generates a $100 Base Rate.

  6. Specify an Overage Rate - this is the hourly rate your customer is billed if they exceed their retainer hours.
  7. Save Service Item.

Money retainer

Create a money retainer service item to generate a block of money you can deduct from as you open tickets over the course of a billing period. Choose to bill your customer up front, before beginning work, or after tickets have been completed. A money retainer is a great way to keep your customers on a budget. After the money retainer is used up, you can generate another block of money and send your customer a bill before doing anymore work. Continue opening tickets and tracking time after your customer pays for another retainer.

  1. Click Customers > Select a customer in the list.
  2. Select Service items > Add a New Service Item > Money Retainer.



  3. Select a Retainer period.
  4. Select a Period Grouping option to share the money across billing periods.
    1. This behaves similar to the Hourly Retainer. Select Quarterly to share the money over a three month period.
    2. For example, a $1,500 money retainer with a monthly retainer period will generate a block of $1,500 each month.
    3. Your customer can use up to $4,500 ($1,500 x 3 months) of the retainer prior to the end of the quarter before overage is applied.
    4. Overage is automatically calculated based on the hourly rates you select when tracking time in tickets. It appears as a separate line item on your customer's invoice if they exceed $4,500 before the end of the quarter.

    With no period grouping set, overage is automatically applied if your customer exceeds their retainer amount during the specified retainer period.

  5. Select a Default Rate, and choose additional hourly rates if needed. These will all be available when adding time entries to tickets.
  6. Click Save Service Item.

If you bill your customer prior to starting work, select Customers > Service items and click the money retainer service item in the list to expand and keep track of how much money your customer has left on their contract through out the month.

Flat fee

Flat fee service items are used for recurring managed services, like server monitoring or monthly anti-virus support. Charge your customers a fixed price multiplied by the number of supported devices in their network. For instance, you charge $50 per server for monthly monitoring and your customer has 6 servers on their network. Using a flat fee service item, MSP Manager automatically creates a monthly bill of $300, without opening any tickets.

  1. Click Customers > Select a customer in the list.
  2. Select Service items > Select Flat Fee as the service item type.
  3. Select Monthly as the billing period.
  4. Enter $50 as the Unit Price.
  5. Enter 6 as the Quantity.

    6. Select Use Asset Count to update the service item quantity automatically using the Integrate N-central with MSP Manager

  6. Select an Additional Rate or use Add New Rate to create a new one.

    7. Flat fee service items have a default rate of $0 to use when creating tickets for issues included in the scope of support. They are an all you can eat type of contract - your customer pays a monthly fee for monitoring and supporting devices in their network, which includes different types of support. Add an hourly rate to the service item to use when opening tickets for anything not included in the scope of support, like after hours emergency support, for instance.

  1. Click Save Service Item.

Project service items

You can create service items to bill your customers for project-based work. Charge your customers a one-time fee for the entire project, or bill by the hour with one or more hourly rates. Generate invoices prior to starting work or create them after the project is completed.

Configure a project service item using multiple hourly rates

In this example, you'll configure a project service item with a total estimated cost of $1,500, 15 estimated hours of work, and multiple hourly rates.

  1. Click Customers, and click a customer in the list.
  2. Click Service Items > Add a New Service Item.
  3. Select Project from the Service Type list.
  4. Type $1500 in the Project Estimate field.
  5. Type 15 in the Estimated Hours field.
  6. Select Actual Hours in the Bill For field.

    7. Actual Hours uses hourly rates to bill your customer for the exact number of hours they use during the course of the project.

  1. Select an hourly rate from the Default Rate list, or use the Add A New Rate button to create a new hourly rate.
  2. Select additional hourly rates from the Additional Rates list.
  3. Click Create Service Item.

The new service item is now assigned to the customer for whom it was created and can be applied to new tickets. Open and complete tickets in order to bill your customers.

Configure a project service item using a one-time fee

In this example, you'll configure a project service item with a one-time project estimate fee of $700, 10 estimated hours, and an additional hourly rate for any work not included in the estimate. This is the best way to set up.

  1. Follow steps 1 - 3 above.
  2. Type $700 in the Project Estimate field.
  3. Type 10 in the Estimated Hours field.

    4. Estimated hours must be in whole numbers with no decimals.

  1. Select Project Estimate in the Bill For field.

    2. Project Estimate bills your customer a one-time fee for all work included in the project.

  1. Select an hourly rate from the Default Rate field.
  2. Click Create Service Item.

Create a new billing batch to generate an invoice and bill your customer up front for the estimated hours. Open tickets to bill for time at the attached hourly rate after the estimated hours are used up.
