Scheduling video script

Use the scheduling section of to add an appointment to your calendar as a reminder to look into an issue or call a customer about a support issue. Appointments typically represent things like on-site visits, remote desktop troubleshooting sessions, or a follow-up phone calls. Schedules are containers for appointments, but appointments can stand alone on their own.

You can also create schedules that contain recurring tickets and appointments for your customers. Recurring tickets are a great way to track time for monthly proactive maintenance, or regular tasks that technicians need to complete on a daily or weekly basis. Tickets are automatically created based on the recurrence frequency selected during creation, and notifications are sent to alert technicians when a new ticket has been assigned to them.

Let's start with creating an appointment

  1. Navigate to the Scheduling section of your account and click Appointments.
  2. In the top right corner, select Add Appointment. Fill out the Appointment Information by assigning a user and a customer to the appointment, and entering a subject. You can also optionally add a location or enter additional details about the appointment.
  3. In the Appointment Scheduling section of the appointment editor, select a start and end date for the appointment.
  4. In the Appointment Association section, you have the option to link a ticket to the appointment. Use the search field find the ticket number or title, select the tickets you want to associate to the appointment and click Save.

You can now view the new appointment in your appointments calendar, and drag and drop any appointments to a new time if needed.

Now let's create a schedule

Schedules are used to create recurring tickets and appointments for your customers. You can use recurring tickets to track time for monthly proactive maintenance, or regular tasks your technicians need to complete. Tickets are automatically created based on the recurrence frequency, and notifications alert employees when a new ticket has been assigned to them.

  1. Navigate to the Scheduling section of your account and click Schedules.
  2. In the top right corner, select Add Schedule, enter the Schedule Title and Details, and select a customer.
  3. In the Schedule Properties section, specify a Recurrence (daily, weekly, monthly, yearly) for the schedule. Depending on the Recurrence option that is selected, additional options are available, like the day of the week and time of day, for instance. Enter the additional information and specify a time zone.
  4. Select a Start and End date for when the schedule begins and ends.
  5. In the Additional Items section of the Schedule editor, you have the option to add a recurring ticket or appointment to the schedule.
    • Click Add Recurring Ticket and fill out the Ticket Schedule Title, Details, Issue Type and Customer information fields. Save the Recurring Ticket to add it to the schedule.
    • Click Add Recurring Appointment and fill out the Appointment Information and Appointment Scheduling sections. Save the Recurring Appointment to add it to the schedule.
  6. Save the schedule to make it active.

As the newly added schedule continues to execute over the course of the next weeks and months, you can use the Audit Logs section of the scheduling editor to view the type of action and details of the schedule. This shows technicians when the schedule was successfully executed and when the next execution will take place.
