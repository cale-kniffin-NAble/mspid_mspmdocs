Track your work with helpdesk tickets

Manage your helpdesk with 's intuitive ticketing and time tracking system, designed for service providers with many customers.

Add a new helpdesk ticket

  1. Click Helpdesk.
  2. Click Add a New Ticket.
  3. Enter the ticket information.
  4. Click Save Ticket, or Save and Start a Timer to start tracking your time.
  5. To add additional information to a ticket, click to the right of the ticket in the Helpdesk to access a list of ticket options.

The ticket subject has a limit of 256 characters, and truncates anything over that limit.

Billable vs. non billable tickets

When the Billable check box of a ticket is selected, and the ticket is marked complete, you can run a billing batch to invoice your customer for all billable rates assigned to each time entry in the ticket.

You can also clear the Billable check box for work that is completed but will not to be charged. The non billable ticket still appears in your billing batch after it is marked complete, but it has a $0 associated to it. This is a way to show your customer that the work was completed at no charge.

Search for tickets

Searching using the default search mechanism (without the '#') will only search within the current filter context. For example, the default list of tickets is 'Open Tickets', which is a filter on tickets with statuses 'Assigned', 'In Progress' and 'On Hold'. In the example provided, ticket 9081 currently is in status 'Reviewed', so it's not actually part of that view. So attempting to search returns no records.

Switching to 'Completed Tickets', ticket 9081 is included in that view.

The major difference when including the '#' prepend is that it searches for that exact ticket number, no matter its status, and opens the ticket editor for that ticket.
