Create and assign service level agreements

Create service level agreements to specify the amount of time expected to create tickets and resolve issues for your customers. Three levels of alerts trigger notifications to remind technicians when a ticket is approaching a breach in SLA. Use your company business hours or specify custom hours to put an SLA policy on hold when business is closed.

Add a new service level agreement (SLA) policy

  1. Click > Account Settings > SLA Policies.
  2. Click Add a New SLA Policy.
  3. Enter a policy name and description.
  4. In the Alert Thresholds area, expand an alert level and define the following:
    • The percentage to breach when the alert is triggered.
    • The color displayed on the associated tickets.
    • The users to receive email notifications.

      • The percentage reaches 200 to accommodate a breach of up to twice the length of the SLA policy.

  5. In the Policy Settings area click the Default Setting and enter a time in hours for each ticket status.

    Repeat this step for each priority section that requires adjustments.

  6. Click Save.

Specify hours to put service level agreements on hold

During non-business hours, SLA timers stop running while your company is closed and continue when business hours resume.

  1. Click > Account Settings > SLA Policies > Edit a policy.
  2. Click each Policy Setting to expand additional options.
  3. Select Only use business hours when calculating SLA.
  4. Select each day in the list and choose an Open at and Close at time.

  5. Select Use default company business hours to use the hours specified in your default account settings.

  1. Click Save Setting.

Go to Account Settings > Default Settings to specify your Company Business Hours.

Assign an SLA policy to a service item

  2. Click Service Items.
  3. Click next to a service item.
  4. In the SLA area, select an SLA Policy.
  5. Click Save Service Item.

Tickets that have service items with assigned SLA policies will send notifications based on your alert thresholds.
