Automate your helpdesk with workflows and routing rules

Create workflows and routing rules to automate your helpdesk and eliminate the risk of human error in your ticketing processes. Workflows consist of a specific order of rules that are initiated by trigger events. These rules are made up of conditions and actions, and can be arranged to create the most efficient workflow for your business.

Triggers are events caused by either the system, users, or technicians. They initiate and execute the actions of the workflow when the specified conditions are met. Actions can automatically create tickets or send notifications any time your customers experience an outage in their network. There are a different set of conditions and actions available for each trigger type.

When workflow is initially enabled, ticket routing is still in effect. As a best practice, create a Customer Access Group for Workflows and add one or two customers to it to test the feature and determine if your rules are set up like you prefer. Then add additional customers to the Access Group after you are satisfied with testing the feature.

Create a Workflow Access Group

When workflow is initially enabled, ticket routing is still in effect. As a best practice, create a Workflow Access Group and add one or two customers to it to test the feature and determine if your rules are set up like you prefer. Then add additional customers to the Access Group after you are satisfied with testing the feature.

  1. Click Settings > Ticket Routing > Workflow Access Groups.
  2. Click Add to create the new Access Group.
  3. Enter a name for the Access Group and select the Workflow Event Type.
  4. Choose the customers you would like to add to the Access Group.
  5. Enable the Access Group when you are ready to turn on Workflows and Rules for the selected customers.
  6. Save the Access Group.

Create a Workflow

  1. Navigate to Account Settings > Workflow & Routing Rules.
  2. Select Add Workflow.
  3. Click Account Settings > Workflow & Routing Rules.
  4. Click Settings > Ticket Routing > Workflow & Rules.
  5. Click Add Workflow.
  6. Enter the workflow title and details.
  7. Select a category. You can choose from three categories:
    1. RMM Outage - an outage for a specific asset from your RMM account.
    2. Incoming Email - an email sent by a customer for a specific issue.
    3. Portal Ticket Request - a customer reported issue sent from the customer portal.
  8. Select a trigger.
  9. Save the workflow.

After the workflow is created and saved, you are ready to add rules to it.

Add rules to your Workflow

  1. Click to expand the new Workflow, and click Add Rule.
  2. Enter the rule title and details.
  3. Click the condition to expand it's options.

  4. Select And/Or, and choose an Attribute.

  5. Select a condition operator (equals, contains, starts with), and enter a value.
  6. Click to add more conditions or condition blocks to the rule.

    7. Condition blocks create a new set of brackets in your rule. For example: [Condition 1 AND Condition 2] OR [Condition 3 AND Condition 4] - In this example, Condition 1 and 2 are in the same Condition block, Condition 3 and 4 are in a different block, and they are separated by the OR Condition.

  7. Click Actions and select an action from the list.
  8. Click Edit next to the new action to specify additional criteria. For example, editing the Assign Technician action allows you to assign the technician based on Customer or Location Assigned Users, User Skills, or you can select specific users.

    9. Create Ticket and Do Not Create Ticket are not editable actions.

  9. Click to confirm the addition of the action to the rule.

  11. Enter the rule title and details.
  12. Click the condition to expand it's options.

  15. Click to add more conditions or condition blocks to the rule.

    16. Condition blocks create a new set of brackets in your rule. For example: [Condition 1 AND Condition 2] OR [Condition 3 AND Condition 4] - In this example, Condition 1 and 2 are in the same Condition block, Condition 3 and 4 are in a different block, and they are separated by the OR Condition.

  16. Click to expand the new Workflow, and click Add Rule.
  17. Enter the rule name and details.
  18. Click the condition to expand it's options.


  19. Click And/Or, and choose an Attribute. Attributes differ depending on the type of workflow. Outage workflows contain attribute options for asset or outage details, ticket workflows contain attribute options for customer and email subject/body information, and Portal ticket request contains options for the contact email, ticket category, details or title.


  20. Select a condition operator (equals, not equal (!=), contains, includes, starts with) and enter a value.
  21. Click to add more conditions or condition blocks to the rule.

    22. Condition blocks create a new set of brackets in your rule using OR instead of AND. For example: [Condition 1 AND Condition 2] OR [Condition 3 AND Condition 4] - In this example, Condition 1 and 2 are in the same Condition block, Condition 3 and 4 are in a different block, and they are separated by the OR Condition.

  22. Click Actions and select an action from the list.
  23. Click to confirm the addition of the action to the rule. You can also drag an item from the Actions list into the Actions section of the Workflow editor.
  24. Click next to the Action to edit and view it's additional criteria. For example, editing the Assign Technician action allows you to assign the technician based on Customer or Location Assigned Users, User Skills, or you can select specific users.

    25. Create Ticket and Do Not Create Ticket are not editable actions.

  25. Continue to add rules to your workflow to cover additional scenarios, and save the rule when it is complete.
