Use ticket routing to automatically assign inbound tickets

Configure ticket routing rules so that email requests or N-central alerts automatically create tickets in . Ticket requests are generated from email, the customer portal, or N-central. Specify a default service item for your customers, and with ticket routing rules in place, turns these emailed requests and alerts into tickets.

Email ticket routing

In order to route a ticket from email, determines the identity of the customer in one of two ways:

  • If the sender email address matches a customer contact.
  • If the sender email domain matches a customer email domain.

Assign an email domain to your customers

Enter an email domain for each customer in for ticket routing to work correctly.

  1. Click Customers, and select a customer in the list.
  2. Click Edit Customer.
  3. Enter the customer domain in the Email Domain field.
  4. Click Save Changes.

Tickets are created from incoming email after assigning an email domain and specifying a default service item.

Specify a default service item

  1. Click Customers, and click a customer in the list.
  2. Click Service Items.
  3. Click to edit a service item.
  4. Select the Default Service Item check box.
  5. Click Save Service Item.

Complete these steps for all customers in for ticket routing to work for everyone.

Portal ticket routing

Customers with portal access must have a default service item specified for tickets to be created automatically in .
